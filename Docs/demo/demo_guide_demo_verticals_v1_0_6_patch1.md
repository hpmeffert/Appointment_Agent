# Demo Verticals v1.0.6 Patch 1 Demo Guide

Version: v1.0.6-patch1
Audience: Demo Presenter
Language: EN

## Simple presenter message

`With one click I can change the full appointment story, not just one headline.`

## Best demo order

1. Start with `Dentist`
2. Continue with `Stadtwerke Wallbox`
3. Finish with `District Heating`

This order works well because the audience first sees a very simple case and then more technical ones.

## What to say for `Dentist`

Simple explanation:

`This is a dental practice example. The titles, reminder text, and customer wording now sound like a real everyday appointment flow.`

Things to point at:

- titles like `Dental Check-up - Dr. Zahn`
- customer names like `Anna Becker`
- reminder wording that sounds friendly and familiar

## What to say for `Stadtwerke Wallbox`

Simple explanation:

`Now the same product becomes a municipal utility or field-service story. The system shows technician visits, inspections, and home appointments.`

Things to point at:

- titles like `Wallbox Inspection - Home Visit`
- customer context like a household with a wallbox installation
- reminder text about a technician visit

## What to say for `District Heating`

Simple explanation:

`This version is more technical. It fits city utilities, infrastructure teams, or building service providers.`

Things to point at:

- titles like `District Heating Transfer Station Inspection`
- customer context like `Hausverwaltung Nordblick`
- wording that sounds more technical and operational

## Parameters you may mention in a live demo

### `vertical`

This chooses the business story.

### `timeframe`

This chooses how far into the future demo appointments are generated.

### `count`

This chooses how many appointments are prepared for the demo.

### `action`

This chooses whether the operator creates, removes, or refreshes the demo data.

### `mode`

This chooses between safe simulation data and the Google test calendar.

## One strong closing line

`The appointment engine stays the same, but the business wording, customer context, and monitoring story adapt to the audience.`
